About:

I am a PhD student in the Computational Science and Engineering field at UC Santa Barbara. My research interest lies in the computational multi-scale phenomenology. I undertake a computational standpoint in order to unravel the complexities that arise in many-body systems whose participant elements range a wide spectrum from atoms, cells, humans, and galaxies. In my research, I enjoy working with the state-of-the-art algorithms for numerically integrating governing PDEs in a parallel environment and on an adaptive mesh (both Cartesian and Voronoi) using the level-set method. We are specifically focused to the class of phenomena where sharp interface physics plays a pivotal role in the overall evolution of complexities and patterns.

I research under supervision of Prof. Frederic Gibou at the CASL group at UCSB.

Before joining UCSB, I spent three years at UC Riverside at the Physics department. During that time I researched the assembly of dwarf galaxies within the Illustris hydrodynamical and cosmological simulations.
