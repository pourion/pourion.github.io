About Me

I am a GIScientist with engineering background and experience in GIS development. I am a PhD student in GIScience at University of California Santa Barbara. As a graduate student researcher, I work in Center for Spatial Studies at UCSB. I hold a bachelor degree in Geodesy and Geomatics Engineering, and a Master of Science degree in GIS engineering, both from K.N.Toosi University of Technology, Tehran, Iran. Here at UCSB Geography, I study how to ask and answer spatio-temporal questions under supervision of Professor Werner Kuhn, and my goal is to facilitate Question-Based Spqtial Computing. My focus is on Field as a Core Concept of Spatial Information and I try to develop Core Computations on fields by extending Map Algebra operations from rastre to field. I am always passionate to develop GIS services and software, provide GIS solutions, and to work on challenging and real world spatial problems.

If you want to know more about the projects I've done, check my Github, and feel free to shoot me an email if you want to contact me.

My Projects

GauchoSafe Android App

This project exploit the potential of smartphones as sensors to report crimes or snese of unsafety for UCSB students. Users of the app can enable it when they feel unsafe, and the app will report their location anonymously to campus officials or UCSB police department. In case of a danger, users can shake their phone, and it will send their location and contact information directly to the police. The location data submitted by users are used to create a heat map of the locations where are felt to be more unsafe.

Technology Stack: Android, Google Maps Api

GauchoSafe Web Interface

This project is a web interface for GauchoSafe app. It provides a heatmap of the locations reported by app users, and police officials can see a detailed report of the locations and also emergency location reports by logging in the website.

Technology Stack: HTML, JavaScript, PHP, Leaflet.js

Core Concepts of Spatial Information

The Core Concepts of Spatial Information are designed to facilitate spatial computing and reduce its complexity. They also serve as conceptual lenses on environments, allowing for different perspectives on them, fed by data with any sort of spatial reference. We specify the Core Concepts as Abstract Data Types (ADT), defining a set of core computations for each concept, through which users can ask spatial questions (Kuhn & Ballatore, 2015). The ultimate goal is a generic Application Programming Interface (API) for spatial computing.

Earthquake Explorer

The Earthquake Explorer app is an event-based interface that offers users a single point-of-entry for access to USGS Earthquakes Hazards Program data as they become available in near-real time. Users can view and get information about earthquake events by time and magnitude. Queries based on spatial extent, defined by place-name or bounding box, can be applied to the data. Summary reports are generated for all earthquake events, along with the selected events in the user-defined area of interest, and are available for export.

Technology Stack: Java, GeoJSON, OSM.
