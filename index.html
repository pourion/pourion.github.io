About me:

I am a PhD student in the Computational Science and Engineering field at UC Santa Barbara. My research interest lies in the computational multi-scale phenomenology. I undertake a computational angle in order to tackle the scientific challenges in many different disciplines from atoms, cells, humans, and galaxies.

I research under supervision of Prof. Frederic Gibou at the CASL group at UCSB.

Before joining UCSB, I spent three years at UC Riverside at the Physics department. During that time I researched the assembly of dwarf galaxies within the context of computational astrophysics.
