About:

I am a PhD candidate in the Computational Science and Engineering field at UC Santa Barbara. My research interest lies in the computational multi-scale phenomenology. I undertake a computational standpoint in order to unravel the complexities that arise in many-body systems whose participant elements range a wide spectrum from atoms, cells, humans, and galaxies. In my research, I enjoy working with the state-of-the-art algorithms for numerically integrating governing PDEs in a parallel environment and on an adaptive mesh (both Cartesian and Voronoi) using the level-set method. We are specifically focused on the class of phenomena in which sharp interface physics plays a pivotal role in the evolution of complexities and patterns.
