About me:

I am a PhD candidate in Computational Science and Engineering at UC Santa Barbara. My research interest is in development of multi-scale simulations and modeling of various physical processes. Specifically I am interested in physical phenomena where a sharp boundary plays a critical role. Examples include (i) direct numerical simulations of multicellular systems where cell membranes are the key regulating factor, and also (ii) direct numerical simulations of epitaxial growth where freely moving boundaries of atomic islands determines resulting patterns emerging in the system (viz. morphology of mounds, etc.). Our computational paradigm is three-fold: (i) we use level-set methods in (ii) parallel environments and on (iii) adaptive grids. This particular combination of computational technologies is the enabling factor in precise simulations of multiscale phenomena with sharp boundaries. The modeling phase follows the code development phase and performing computational experiments on national supercomputers. We are particularly interested in statistical physical tools in order to develop models based on the datasets we produce. Another emerging aspect is the application of machine learning methods to infer models from our large-scale computational experiments.
