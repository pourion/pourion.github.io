Pouria received his PhD in Mechanical Engineering with an emphasis on Computational Science and Engineering (CSE) from University of California Santa Barbara in 2020, and his MS degree in Physics with an emphasis in computational astrophysics from University of California Riverside in 2014. Prior to that, he received two B.S. degrees in Aerospace Engineering and Physics from Sharif University of Technology in Iran in 2013. Pouria’s research interest is in developing mathematical models and building scientific computing frameworks for in-silico investigations of multiscale phenomena, and the interdependence between system behaviors at different scales. In his career, he has worked on computational modeling of several complex systems including galaxy clusters, cell aggregates, and atomic islands. Before joining Silicon Therapeutics, he was in a postdoctoral program at Merck Research Laboratories where he developed a computational model for long-term simulations of physical instabilities in biotherapeutic formulations. In his current role, Pouria will be working as part of the platform team to develop and optimize computational algorithms for the physics-based drug discovery simulation framework.
